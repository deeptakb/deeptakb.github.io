Karma

Action and impression

  • Blog
Karma is the actions we do. But this is a little more subtle than that. Karma, in some sense, implies the impressions or impact left on the system performing the action. The impressions we are talking about are a result of the past action we have performed. Here, we will discuss as to why should we perform the karma and how does it leaves us with heaps of impressions. We will also see as what can be done about it.

We perform a certain karma because we want to do that. Or in other words it is the desire which drives us to perform a certain karma. We need to dwell in a further on this desire issue to get a more broader view on the things involved. Desire is something that makes us do things, takes us to places and defines the way we are to a great extent. I am not writing this post through some favorite editor of mine but through the desire to write and communicate with others. So desires seems like a nice things, which makes us do things which can be helpful for oneself and others, hopefully!

There are two important aspects to our desires - material and spiritual. It is the material desire which will not satiate and hence we will keep pining for what is not! This may steal our 'inner peace' leading us to, erroneously, believe that all sorts of sufferings find their seeds in desires. Then, one starts desiring that all desires should go for the suffering to cease. And one only outgrow the desires, when they try to kill them. But once we start focusing our energies on the other spiritual aspect of desires, inherent in us, we will start unraveling other dimensions of life which brings more peace and understanding to our system.

The karma of a person engrossed in materialistic desires has a huge heap of impressions of the past collected over time, like - my car, my home, my wife, my body, my mind, etc. But once we start putting in directed effort on the spiritual side we see that the even the mind which contains the past karma in form of memory or impression is actually perverting our view on things. We are not actually unbiased on things if we are driven by a brain tainted with memories.

Memory, again, is a good thing to have, but then we must be able to keep it a distance once we are done playing with it. We should be able to make a distance with whatever is physical! This is what will give us taintless eye on things and hence a view to see things, the way they are, and not corrupted in any way.

May the force be with you!
