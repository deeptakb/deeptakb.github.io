Research

Experiments like LHC in CERN and RHIC, AGS from BNL have been performing collisions between various ions and especially heavier ones to understand the phase diagram of Quantum Chromo Dynamics (QCD). In these collisions, the nuclear matter undergoes a phase transition (like in RHIC for finite baryon density) or smooth crossover (LHC, zero baryon density) to a state of deconfined quarks and gluons. This matter experiences a rapid expansion and cools down and ultimately freezes out. Hydrodynamics is a useful tool to study evolution, whereas various statistical and thermal models are assigned to understand the freeze-out characteristics. With both these approaches, we can work without taking into account the microscopic details of the fireball. Presently I am motivated in these phenomenological aspects of the QCD in the heavy-ion collision.
